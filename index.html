Understanding

What is
mental health?

Mental health is the foundation of how we think, feel, and connect with the world. It shapes every decision we make, every relationship we build, and every challenge we face.

Despite its importance, mental health remains deeply misunderstood. Mental illness is not weakness — it's a health condition, just like any other, and it deserves the same care and attention.

MindSpace was built to close the gap between people in need and the support they deserve — starting with free, accessible tools and growing with you as your needs evolve.

Breathing Tool

Calm your
nervous system.

Guided breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system in minutes. Choose a technique and follow the rhythm.

🧠
Activates the Vagus Nerve

Slow breathing shifts your body from fight-or-flight to rest-and-digest within minutes.

❤️
Lowers Heart Rate

Controlled exhales measurably reduce resting heart rate and blood pressure.

🌙
Improves Sleep

The 4-7-8 technique reduces time to fall asleep by calming pre-bed rumination.

💡 Pro Tip

Try breathing through your nose only — nasal breathing filters air and produces nitric oxide, a natural vasodilator.

🌿
tap to start

Complete 5 cycles for maximum benefit

📦
Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

Used by Navy SEALs to stay calm under pressure. Equal counts in, hold, out, hold.

😴
4-7-8 Relaxation

Dr. Andrew Weil's sleep technique. Longer exhale activates your parasympathetic system.

⚖️
Equal Breathing (5-5)

Best for beginners. Balanced in-out rhythm brings instant mental equilibrium.

🌿 When to use this

Before sleep, during a panic attack, before a difficult conversation, or any moment you need to slow down.

Common Distortions

The 8 most common
thinking traps

Click any card to load an example into the reframing tool. Recognizing these patterns is half the battle.

01
All-or-Nothing Thinking

Seeing everything as black or white — success or failure, good or bad, with no middle ground.

"I always fail at everything."
02
Catastrophizing

Assuming the worst possible outcome in every situation, no matter how unlikely.

"Everything is going to fall apart."
03
Labelling

Applying a fixed, negative label to yourself or others based on one event.

"I'm a loser. Nobody likes me."
04
Overgeneralization

Drawing sweeping conclusions from a single negative event.

"I never do anything right."
05
Mind Reading

Assuming you know what others are thinking — usually something negative.

"They think I'm incompetent."
06
Should Statements

Rigid rules about how you or others "must" behave, leading to guilt and frustration.

"I should always be productive."

Use the prompts tab for inspiration, or write freely. Your entries are saved locally and never leave your device.

🧘
Reduces rumination

Writing a thought down stops your brain from looping it endlessly through your mind.

🔍
Surfaces hidden patterns

Over weeks, your journal becomes a mirror — showing triggers and what lifts you.

💪
Builds emotional resilience

Naming your emotions gives you power over them. Research calls this "affect labeling."

Self-Care

Daily habits for a
healthier mind.

01 ·

Move Your Body

Just 20–30 minutes of daily movement releases endorphins. A walk counts. Consistency beats intensity every time — show up even on the hard days.

02 ·

Protect Your Sleep

7–9 hours of quality sleep is non-negotiable. Create a wind-down routine and keep a consistent schedule — your brain heals and resets during sleep.

03 ·

Mindful Breathing

The 4-7-8 technique activates your parasympathetic nervous system in minutes. Try it before bed or during moments of stress throughout the day.

04 ·

Limit Doom-Scrolling

Set a 30-minute daily social media limit. Replace one scroll session with something that genuinely fills your cup — a walk, a book, a real conversation.

05 ·

Connect Authentically

Deep conversations are more restorative than many shallow ones. Quality always matters more than quantity when it comes to human connection.

06 ·

Journal Your Thoughts

Writing externalizes your thoughts, reducing their weight and emotional charge. Even 5 minutes a day creates meaningful clarity and self-understanding.
